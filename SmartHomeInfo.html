The History of Smart Homes

Smart homes started, as most things do, with ideas. Smart homes appeared in science fiction mostly in caution, but today the application of it is largely considered a convince. The foundation of smart homes started back in the early 1900s with the use of appliances like the first engine-powered vacuum, though we wouldn't classify that as a 'smart' appliance. In 1966-1967 appliances like the ECHO IV and the Kitchen Computer were developed but never hit the markets. 1991 marked a new age in research into Gerontechnology, combining gerontolgy and technology in an effort to make the lives of senior citizens easier. True smart homes didn't gain popularity until the late 1990s and early 2000s. Today our smart homes are often oriented towards security and saving energy.

Should I Make My Home Smarter?

Pros

Increase the convience of your home. Change lighting, temperture, and more without leaving the couch with one device.
Save time and money. Come home to a warm house in the winter or a cool house in the summer without leaving your A/C or heater on all day while you're away, you can just change the settings of your home on your commute back. This saves you energy and hassle.
Be notified if something is wrong. Know if someone unexpected is moving around outside or opening doors and windows. This can help prevent burglries and idenify thieves.
Know when your fridge is empty and keep track of how long those eggs or leftovers have been in there.
Smart homes can better cater to disabled persons and the older generations. Without having to move rooms to complete tasks or look for keys they can benefit greatly.
Most smart homes can be set up to be more eco-friendly!
Open doors for guest remotely. It can be a pain to arrange your schedale to meet with repair or other maintenace people, but with a smart lock you can stay at work.
Some insurance companies even offer discounts to owners of smart homes since they recognize them as less likely to be broken into



Cons

Smart homes can have high istallation costs.
A reliable internet connetion is a must with smart homes, so if you live in an area with poor internet connection than you are likely to experience a lot of issues getting devices to respond.
Integrating technology into your home brings security risks. Hackers can get access to your smart home system to unlock doors to steal your things or steal your data.
If you're not used to the technology it can be a chore to learn to get used to it.
There are privacy concerns when involving third parties who may gather your information
The technology may become outdated soon considering thed process of AI and machine learning.
Smart homes are vulnerable to technical problems. If you use the device often you can become relient on them and feel lost when they fail.
If your home system is set up poorly things may not happen as they should. The voice control might not recognized your voice so you can get locked out of your home and be forced to contact an expert to open your door which can cost be costly
