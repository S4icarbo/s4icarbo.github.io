Smart Homes


Smart Homes, or home automation, is a system of interent connected devices and appliances that can be controlled often remotely. Typically the devices will be connected to a smart home hub, but not always. A smart home hub (or smart hub, gateway, bridge, controller, or coordinator) in what allows the different componets of a smart home to communicate and respond back to each other through a central point.

Big Players in Smart Homes


Google Nest

Including Pixel, Chromebooks, Fitbit devices, and other hardware Google Nest had a revenue of $4.4 billion. Some of its most popular smart home items are the Nest Mini, Nest Hub and Nest Hub Max, Nest thermostat, Nest Cam, and the Nest Protect smoke alarm.


Amazon

When people think of smart speakers most think of Amazon's Echo or Echo Dot. By 2019 Amazon had passed a combined 100 million Alexa-powered devices sold. Many connected home product compaines support the use of Alexa for voice activation and control. Amazon also leads in smart bulbs, door locks, security systems and kitchen appliances. Its website is the most populr superstore for Iot items.


Apple HomeKit

Apple HomeKit is another large seller of smart items including bulbs, locks, motion sensors, room monitors, outlets, switches, and thermostats, but do not include Apple smartwatches. Many brands that fall under Apple HomeKit are third-party companies that have reoganized to be dedicated to Apple.


Ecobee

Ecobee is one of the more recognizable brands especially in its base country Canada, despite offering comparitively fewer products. Their most popular product is their ecobee3 lite which applies voice control to a smart thermostat.


Bosch Smart Home Solutions

Bosch leads the market in Europe with their devisions in heating and lighting, the Philips Hue lights making up a significant portion of that, along with their 360-dgree cameras, video doorbells, and kitchen appliances.


Expected Change

As companies focused in Iot expand they are expected to consolidate. Currently larger compaines like Apple, Amazon, Google, and Bosch are buying up innovative startups.
